# Agents as Coworkers (Not Features)

## Definition

[[mckinsey]]'s 2026 framing of agentic AI in the workforce: agents are **coworkers**, not features inside a platform. The unit of analysis for AI ROI is the human-agent team, not the agent-equipped product. Sourced from [[mckinsey-agentic-ai-workforce-redesign-2026-05-18]] (*"Redesigning Technology Workforce for the Agentic AI Era,"* 2026-04-06): *"infuse agentic AI throughout their organizations to create a true hybrid human-agent workforce, with effective change management to ensure people embrace this shift."*

## Why It Matters

This frame is **subtly disruptive to every IC-platform competitor pitching an agentic-suite-inside-a-platform** — Firstup's five-pillar agentic suite, Staffbase's AI Navigator, [[simpplr]]'s Comms AI, [[workshop]]'s Cici, Poppulo's Create/Target/Analyze Agents. If a CHRO/CIO reads McKinsey through this lens, they don't want a platform with *embedded* agents; they want their workforce — including the IC team — to *operate alongside* agents that may live anywhere (Copilot, ChatGPT Enterprise, custom agents in Azure AI Foundry, etc.). The platform-as-agent-container pitch becomes legacy framing.

For Cerkl, the frame is *opportunity*: the "delivery + measurement layer" thesis fits *agents-as-coworkers* in a way that platform-as-agent-container does not. Cerkl can be the **measurement of how AI-narrative comms actually land** — not the source of agentic capability, but the substrate proving change-comms reaches the right employee on the right channel.

## How It Works

McKinsey's frame implies three buyer-side shifts:

1. **From "which platform has the best AI features"** → *"how do we communicate the workforce reshape so people embrace agents as coworkers?"*
2. **From "buy and adopt vendor AI"** → *"hire, reskill, and govern such that agents and humans collaborate."* (The three-pillar framework: talent / capabilities / vendor relationships.)
3. **From "AI ROI = productivity gains"** → *"AI ROI = effective change-management about agents."* The load-bearing McKinsey quote: *"Without a shared understanding of which skills matter most — and top-down change management to communicate how agents can be coworkers — reskilling efforts tend to remain ineffective."*

The frame is **adjacent to but distinct from** [[people-amplification]] ([[gartner]]) — both are anti-substitution. Gartner says *AI ROI comes from amplifying people, not replacing them*. McKinsey says *agents work alongside people, treated as coworkers*. They reinforce; they don't substitute.

The frame is **also a candidate for not sticking as buzzword.** Less clean than "deep listening" or "enablement illusion." More likely to live inside McKinsey's broader "agentic organization" narrative than to escape as standalone language. Track citation velocity through Q3 2026.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **Vague.** *Coworkers* anthropomorphizes agents in a way that creates downstream comms problems — see [[hr-reporter-ai-risks-ic-2026-05-18]] / [[co-piloting]] for the practitioner-side complication. Cardon & Coman's evidence is that AI-assisted relational writing erodes trust; "agent as coworker" arguably encourages exactly the conflation Cardon & Coman caution against.
- **Less buzzword-shaped than competitors' phrasings.** May not escape McKinsey's corpus.
- **The frame depends on McKinsey adoption.** If trade press / analyst peers don't pick it up, it stays niche.
