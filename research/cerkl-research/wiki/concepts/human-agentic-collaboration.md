# Human-Agentic Collaboration

## Definition

Human-agentic collaboration is [[mckinsey]]'s *Rewired 2.0* Lever #4 — the dimension of AI transformation where managers shift from supervising tasks to orchestrating hybrid systems of humans plus AI agents. McKinsey's framing: *"People will need to be upskilled so they can design, operate, and supervise complex workflows."* Managers do *"the emotional work of helping humans feel safe alongside machine colleagues"* alongside *"guardrails, handoffs, and judgment at the edge."*

## Why It Matters

Lever #4 is the most comms-adjacent of the four levers in *Rewired 2.0* — the place where McKinsey explicitly names the emotional and relational work that IC, EX, and frontline managers actually do. The "emotional work" phrasing is the closest a top-tier analyst firm has come in 2026 to naming change-comms as a discrete lever in AI transformation. It's an unusually quotable McKinsey statement and worth treating as the comms-adjacent anchor of *Rewired 2.0* citations.

## How It Works

The lever has two dimensions:

- **Workflow orchestration** — managers design and supervise complex workflows where AI agents are part of the team. Guardrails, handoffs, escalation criteria, and judgment-at-the-edge are explicit management responsibilities.
- **Emotional and relational work** — managers help humans feel safe alongside machine colleagues. This is the change-comms surface: managing identity threat, role anxiety, trust calibration, and the social fabric of teams as agentic capacity expands.

[[bain]]'s 2026 finding adds operational color: when AI is the stated rationale for change, employee understanding of the reorg drops by ≥10 points vs other transformations — making the relational work *more* load-bearing, not less.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **The lever assumes managers have the capacity to do this work.** [[gallup]] data shows manager engagement collapsing 27% → 22% YoY; if managers are themselves disengaged, the emotional-work assumption fails.
- **The "emotional work" phrasing risks being read as soft skill rather than structural work.** Practitioners may dismiss it as motivational language when it's actually naming a load-bearing operational responsibility.
- **Comms is implicit, not explicit.** The lever names manager-as-orchestrator but doesn't name comms infrastructure, channels, listening cadence, or measurement. IC practitioners reading the work have to draw the comms angle out themselves.
