# Change Saturation

## Definition

Change saturation is [[prosci]]'s threshold-model concept for the upper bound of organizational change tolerance: engagement and capacity rise with change up to a cumulative concurrent-events threshold, then invert sharply as employees lose the bandwidth to absorb additional change. The model gives a name to *why* the [[goldilocks-zone-of-change]] has an upper edge.

## Why It Matters

Saturation reframes the IC measurement question from *"is this campaign well-communicated?"* to *"how many concurrent change events is this employee currently absorbing?"* The shift forces IC and HR to track a portfolio-level metric — cumulative concurrent change-events per employee — rather than per-program comms quality. If saturation is real and the threshold can be quantified, it becomes a structural constraint on change-program sequencing, not just a sentiment data point.

## How It Works

[[prosci]]'s public framing is directional:

- Below threshold: each additional change *can* lift engagement if it's investment-coded and listening cadence is steady
- At threshold: marginal change becomes neutral or mildly negative
- Above threshold: relationship inverts sharply — additional change degrades engagement, increases burnout, and reduces trust in leadership

73% of change-affected employees report moderate-to-high stress per Prosci, suggesting most orgs are operating at or above threshold most of the time.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **No published quantitative threshold.** The model is directional. Without a number, it's hard to design against.
- **Threshold likely varies by org, function, and individual.** A frontline retail worker absorbing an RTO + a new POS + a manager change has a different threshold than a back-office engineer on a stable team.
