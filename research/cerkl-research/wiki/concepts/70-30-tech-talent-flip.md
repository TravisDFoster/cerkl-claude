# 70-30 Tech Talent Flip

## Definition

[[mckinsey]]'s *Rewired 2.0* operational rule for the talent-model dimension of AI transformation: **>70% of tech talent should be in-house** (vs outsourced) and **>70% of the IT cohort should be doer-engineers** (vs managers). The two flips are a *Rewired 2.0* addition that wasn't in the 2023 1st edition.

## Why It Matters

The flips give the [[talent-transformation-thesis]] operational specifics. Most leadership conversations on AI transformation default to vendor-and-roadmap framing; the 70-30 flips force the conversation onto in-house capability and engineering-to-management ratio — both of which are slow, structural changes that take quarters to execute and are visible in workforce data. For IC, this matters because it implies the talent comms challenge isn't just "communicate the AI strategy" but "communicate a multi-year workforce restructure that pushes against the prevailing outsource-and-manage default."

## How It Works

Three classes of workforce capacity must now be planned together:

- **People** — the in-house, doer-heavy tech cohort.
- **Agents** — AI capacity treated as a coworker class ([[agents-as-coworkers]]).
- **Physical automation** — where applicable, robotics and embodied automation.

Each class has different reliability, governance, and onboarding profiles, and McKinsey's framing says workforce planning must budget all three together rather than treating agents as features inside platforms.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **The "in-house >70%" rule is hard to reconcile with current outsourcing economics** for many enterprises; McKinsey doesn't quantify the cost of the shift.
- **The "doer >70%" rule maps poorly onto large IT orgs with deep middle-management layers.** The implied restructure is significant and politically expensive — McKinsey understates the change-comms burden of executing the flip itself.
- **No verifiable case studies cited at the 70/30 specifically.** The numbers feel like opinion-coded targets rather than empirically derived thresholds.

## Open Questions

- What's the empirical basis for the 70-30 specifically (vs 60-40 or 80-20)?
- Has any large enterprise publicly committed to and executed the flips under the *Rewired* banner?
