# The Analyst Trio: Communications as the AI Gate

## Definition

A 2026 convergence across three top-tier analyst voices ([[mckinsey]], [[gartner]], [[forrester]]) all arguing that **internal communications is the binding constraint on AI ROI** — from three different angles. Named here on the back of [[mckinsey-agentic-ai-workforce-redesign-2026-05-18]] explicitly recommending the trio synthesis.

## Why It Matters

When three independent analyst firms converge on the same conclusion from three different lenses (workforce design, rollout strategy, listening), the resulting frame is high-leverage for marketing content — *"the analysts agree: communications is the gate to AI ROI"* is harder to dismiss than any single source. For Cerkl, the trio synthesis is the **strongest available external validation** for the [[delivery-as-comms-strategy]] thesis: AI ROI depends on whether the org can communicate the change — and that puts the IC team at the table whether the CHRO/CIO names them or not.

## How It Works

The three analyst frames, one paragraph each:

**1. McKinsey — Workforce-design lens (CIO-fronted)**
[[mckinsey]]'s *Redesigning Technology Workforce for the Agentic AI Era* (2026-04-06) frames agents as **coworkers, not features**, and explicitly names change-comms as the gate: *"Without a shared understanding of which skills matter most — and top-down change management to communicate how agents can be coworkers — reskilling efforts tend to remain ineffective."* CIO-fronted, CHRO co-buyer. See [[agents-as-coworkers]].

**2. Gartner — Rollout-strategy lens (CHRO-fronted)**
[[gartner]]'s 2026-05-13 *People-Centric AI Strategy* release predicts 50% of enterprises will lose top AI talent by 2027 without a people-centric strategy; coined [[enablement-illusion]] (leaders mistake adoption for transformation); 88% of employees with enterprise AI also use personal AI ([[shadow-ai-comms-problem]]). Pairs with the May 2026 *AI Layoffs Don't Deliver Returns* release ([[people-amplification]]: high-ROI cohort invests *more* in people). CHRO-fronted with CIO co-buyer; the rollout-comms half of the AI-as-IC-gate argument.

**3. Forrester — Listening lens (EX-fronted)**
[[forrester]]'s *AI Will Rewrite EX, and Deep Listening Shows How* (Brodeur-Johnson + McQuivey, 2026-02-06) names the category: **[[deep-listening]]** — AI on Teams/meeting collaboration exhaust producing real-time sentiment, replacing surveys. The measurement / feedback half of the same loop: communications is upstream (delivery), listening is downstream (response); AI ROI requires both sides.

**The convergence:** All three say the *organizational ability to communicate about AI* is what determines whether AI delivers value. None of them say "buy a better AI tool." All of them say "fix the comms layer."

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **The trio convergence is partly an artifact of timing.** All three pieces ran in Feb–May 2026; the framing alignment may reflect shared assumptions in 2026 advisory practice rather than independent agreement. Worth flagging when citing externally.
- **None of the three name specific IC platforms.** The "comms is the gate" claim is implicit-grade for Cerkl's category, not explicit-grade. Don't oversell.
- **Trio language may not stick** as a buzzword (the way "deep listening" or "enablement illusion" do). It's a *synthesis* concept, useful for content; less likely to escape as standalone phrasing.
