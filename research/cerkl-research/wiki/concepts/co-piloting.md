# Co-Piloting (Human-Led AI Use)

## Definition

The academic prescription that humans should draft relational content first and use AI only for polish — "human in the center" rather than "AI as primary author." Sourced from [[linjuan-rita-men]] (UF, Public Relations Review) and [[anthony-coman]] (UF Warrington), as synthesized in [[hr-reporter-ai-risks-ic-2026-05-18]]. The frame names what the practitioner survey data (Cardon & Coman 2025, n=1,100) shows: AI assistance erodes perceived authenticity on relational messages — co-piloting is the workflow that preserves authenticity.

## Why It Matters

Co-piloting is the **operational counter-narrative** to the dominant 2026 vendor pitch of *"single-prompt-to-campaign agentic delivery."* Three reasons it matters for the vault:

1. **It is empirically grounded.** Cardon & Coman 2025 (n=1,100) is the statistical anchor; Men et al. (n=20 senior practitioners) is the qualitative companion.
2. **It is the operational frame Cerkl can validate** for Omni AI — *AI for digest assembly / summarization / segmentation; not for drafting the CEO's heartfelt all-hands.*
3. **It separates the AI-skeptic narrative from anti-AI positioning.** Co-piloting is not "don't use AI" — it's "use AI for polish, not as a starting point." That distinction matters for vendors who do ship AI features and want to honor the practitioner trust concern.

## How It Works

Two named practitioner-side prescriptions:

- **[[anthony-coman]]:** *"Begin with your brain and draft with your brain first."* For relational messages, AI is a polish tool, not an authorship tool.
- **[[linjuan-rita-men]]:** *"Co-piloting is the best approach. Enhance the efficiency and efficacy of the work, but always have a human in the center."*

The operational split (from [[hr-reporter-ai-risks-ic-2026-05-18]]):

| Content type | AI's appropriate role | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Transactional / informational (FAQs, policy updates, scheduling) | Lead / draft / generate | Low authenticity stakes; high efficiency upside |
| Relational (recognition, motivation, congratulations) | Polish only; human drafts | Authenticity is the *job to be done* |
| High-stakes (performance reviews, crisis comms) | Stay out | Wrong tool; reputational asymmetry |

**Why the term is loaded.** Microsoft owns "Copilot" as a product brand. Cerkl marketing copy should prefer **"human-in-the-loop"** or **"human-centered AI"** to avoid trademark adjacency and signal independence from the Microsoft framing.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **"Copilot" is a Microsoft brand.** Linguistic adjacency creates conflation risk. Default to "human-in-the-loop" / "human-centered" in Cerkl marketing.
- **The split is not always clean.** Many IC artifacts (newsletter content, all-hands prep, change-comms drafts) blur transactional and relational. The discipline is *deciding* which mode applies per artifact — and that's a meta-skill, not a tool.
- **Co-piloting is harder than agentic delivery.** A vendor pitching "the AI does it all" sells against a vendor pitching "do it yourself, AI helps." The latter is the harder pitch — even if it's the more accurate one.
- **Sample size caveat:** Men et al. is n=20.
