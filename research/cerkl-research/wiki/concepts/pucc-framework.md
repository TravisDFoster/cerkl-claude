# PUCC Framework

## Definition

PUCC — **Predictability, Understanding, Control, Compassion** — is [[robert-sutton]]'s (Stanford / HBR) framework for communicating difficult decisions, especially layoffs. The framework: minimize trauma by telling people what's coming (Predictability), why (Understanding), what choices they have (Control), and showing you see their pain (Compassion).

## Why It Matters

PUCC is the through-line HBR is using in 2025-2026 updates on layoff comms, and the underlying logic anchoring why the 2026 [[ai-restructure-memo-subgenre]]'s "good" templates land and the "bad" ones don't. [[gitlab]]'s "Act 2" memo hits all four dimensions; [[cisco]]'s "Our Path Forward" hits Understanding + Control well but is mid on Compassion (the strong-quarter opener); [[gm]]'s scripted 15-minute call fails on all four. PUCC provides the diagnostic language ICs are now reaching for when grading 2026 memos.

## How It Works

Each dimension is operational:

- **Predictability** — tell people what's coming. Vague is corrosive. Voluntary-separation deadlines (GitLab 2026-05-18, Microsoft 2026-06-06) score well here. 15-minute-out calendar invites (GM) fail.
- **Understanding** — name the why. GitLab's *"we're building software with machines, directed by people"* + GitLab's refusal of euphemism scores well. LinkedIn's official memo (which omits AI) fails.
- **Control** — give people choices. Voluntary-first ([[voluntary-first-default]]) is the highest-control move. Tenure-scaled severance + outplacement (GM's actually competitive package) is mid. Immediate access lockout (Oracle) is zero-control.
- **Compassion** — show you see their pain. GitLab's *"Today is hard. I want to acknowledge how difficult today is given the volume of change we're asking you to take in"* scores high. Cisco's strong-quarter opener undermines compassion even with a strong tangible offer. Oracle's generic *"Oracle Leadership"* signoff is zero-compassion.

Sandra Sucher's HBR 2025-08 reframe extends PUCC to the *workforce transition* — pair the announcement with manager scripts (Predictability + Compassion at the line-manager level), a 24-hour town hall (Understanding), and named survivor support (Control + Compassion for the silent audience).

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **PUCC is graded at the memo, not at the transition.** A memo can score high on PUCC and still fail if there's no manager script, no town hall, no survivor comms.
- **Compassion is the easiest to perform and the hardest to land.** The line between authentic acknowledgment and corporate-platitude language is thin — and 2026 IC trade press is highly attuned to the difference.
- **The framework assumes the decision is fixed.** PUCC doesn't address whether the cut should happen, only how it should be communicated. Critics argue that frames layoff comms as a presentation problem when it's structurally an organizational design problem.
