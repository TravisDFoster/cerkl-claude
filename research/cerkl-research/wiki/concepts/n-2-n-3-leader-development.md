# N-2 / N-3 Leader Development

## Definition

The [[mckinsey]] *Rewired 2.0* Lever #1: building the tech muscle of business leaders specifically at the **N-2 and N-3 layer** — two and three levels below the CEO. The frame says senior executives often have or get AI literacy; the binding constraint is the layer two or three steps down, where domain ownership and operational decision rights sit.

## Why It Matters

The N-2/N-3 framing is operational, not aspirational — McKinsey is naming a specific organizational layer to invest in, with a specific outcome (AI-enabled transformation roadmap, modern software-delivery literacy, appreciation for data-estate/platform/talent health). For IC and leadership-comms, this matters because the N-2/N-3 layer is *also* the manager-cascade layer where IC programs typically struggle most. The development imperative and the comms imperative are pointed at the same population — and a coherent strategy treats them as one program.

## How It Works

McKinsey prescribes targeted development journeys for domain owners. The success criteria:

- Develop an AI-enabled transformation roadmap
- Understand modern software delivery
- Appreciate the health of the data estate, technology platforms, and engineering talent
- Steer teams with an "AI-first sensibility"

The remedy is *targeted dev journeys*, not generic training — implying a per-leader investment.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **The "AI muscle" framing is fuzzy.** What does an AI-literate N-2 actually do that an AI-illiterate N-2 doesn't? McKinsey doesn't operationalize the test.
- **Targeted dev journeys are expensive and slow.** The lever may be correct but unimplementable at scale within the AI-rollout timelines most leaders are working under.
- **Doesn't account for the manager-engagement collapse.** N-2/N-3 leaders are exactly the cohort [[gallup]] shows is disengaging fastest.
