# Listening Cadence

## Definition

Listening cadence is the *frequency* dimension of organizational employee listening — how often the org actually solicits, receives, and responds to feedback channels (pulse surveys, town hall Q&A, 1:1 cadence, comment threads, sentiment exhaust). Distinct from [[deep-listening]], which is about the analytical sophistication of the listening layer, and from [[employee-listening]], which is the broader discipline.

## Why It Matters

[[qualtrics]] named listening cadence — not comms quality — as the moderator of the change-engagement relationship in its 2026 EX Trends Report. The data is sharp: orgs that *increased* listening cadence saw 87% engagement vs 44% for orgs that didn't. **42%** of employees want more frequent listening. This is the rare moderator that's (a) operationally controllable by IC/HR, (b) quantitatively defensible, and (c) decoupled from message content. The precision is load-bearing: trade press repeatedly substitutes "comms quality" for "listening cadence" and loses the empirical claim.

## How It Works

The Qualtrics 2026 framing is directional rather than mechanistic:

- High listening cadence + significant change → 87% composite engagement
- Low listening cadence + significant change → 44% composite engagement
- The lift compounds across EX metrics (up to 4x reported), suggesting cadence functions as a system-level moderator rather than a single-variable effect

Operational tactics implied (not enumerated in primary materials):

- Pulse surveys at higher than monthly cadence
- Manager 1:1 prompts that elicit and route feedback upward
- Town halls with live Q&A (not announcements-only)
- Acting on captured feedback within a visible response window

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **Operationalization is opaque.** Qualtrics doesn't publicly define "listening frequency" in measurable units. Without that, the 87/44 gap is impossible to design against.
- **Cadence without acting on input may be corrosive.** [[justin-black]]'s [[execution-gap]] argues organizations that collect feedback but fail to act break trust faster than orgs that don't collect at all. Cadence is necessary but not sufficient.
- **Over-listening risk.** [[gallagher]]'s State of the Sector 2026 finds over-communicating increases distrust by 30% and burnout by 24% — the same risk may apply to over-soliciting feedback.
