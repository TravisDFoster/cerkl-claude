# HR Reporter: Using AI for Internal Communications? Know the Risks

**Author/Publisher:** [[sarah-dobson]] / [[hr-reporter]]  |  **Published:** 2025-12-08  |  **Source type:** Trade-pub article synthesizing two academic studies  |  **URL:** https://www.hrreporter.com/focus-areas/automation-ai/using-ai-for-internal-communications-know-the-risks/393831

## Summary

A December 2025 [[hr-reporter]] article by staff writer [[sarah-dobson]] anchored on two peer-reviewed academic studies — [[peter-cardon]] & [[anthony-coman]]'s *"Professionalism and Trustworthiness in AI-Assisted Workplace Writing"* (International Journal of Business Communication, 2025; n=1,100 working professionals) and [[linjuan-rita-men]]'s *"Artificial intelligence for internal communication: Strategies, challenges, and implications"* (Public Relations Review; n=20 senior comms professionals). The piece makes a sober counter-case to the AI-maximalist trade press: AI in IC degrades **trust, authenticity, and perceived sincerity** for *relational* content (recognition, motivation, performance reviews, crisis comms), and the prescribed fix is [[co-piloting]] — humans draft, AI polishes — not single-prompt agentic delivery. Paired with [[simpplr]]'s 2026 State of IC findings (52% / 29% / >33%), the article is the academic-anchor half of the [[ai-anxious-buyer]] frame and is load-bearing for the Tensions section on [[ai-in-internal-comms]].

## Key Takeaways

- **The risks are about trust and sincerity, not hallucinations or sentiment-analysis ethics.** Five distinct mechanisms: trust erosion, authenticity loss, reputational harm to sender, skill atrophy, wrong-tool-for-high-stakes scenarios, sincerity decline on relational content.
- **The killer practitioner-side line:** *"If my manager has to use AI to write a message like this, why are they my manager at all?"* — the article's most quotable critique of AI-assisted manager comms.
- **The prescribed solution is [[co-piloting]]** — "human in the center, AI for polish." Not anti-AI; explicitly *human-led* AI use.
- **No vendor named directly.** References "Gmail's suggested replies" and "Microsoft's Outlook Copilot" only. Unusual for trade press — the abstraction-from-vendors is what makes the frame portable.
- **The piece is December 2025**, surfaced into the daily-recap window via Simpplr's 2026 State of IC citation cycle. Worth noting for the recap process — practitioner-sentiment pieces have a longer tail than vendor news.
- **The 52% / 29% / >33% stat trio is not in this article.** That cluster comes from [[simpplr]]'s 2026 State of IC Report — complementary signal, different source. Treat the academic frame (HR Reporter) and the survey data (Simpplr) as paired but distinct anchors.

## Notable Claims

**Risks enumerated:**
1. **Trust erosion** — AI-written messages perceived as less authentic when AI assistance is visible (Cardon & Coman 2025, n=1,100).
2. **Authenticity loss** — *"Lack of human experience and human touch in the communication."*
3. **Reputational harm to sender** — Using AI may damage supervisor credibility and perceived caring.
4. **Skill atrophy** — Over-reliance on AI tools without the expected time savings.
5. **Wrong tool for high-stakes scenarios** — Performance reviews and crisis communications specifically called out.
6. **Sincerity decline for relational content** — Congratulations, motivation, recognition — degraded by visible AI assistance.

**Academic quotes:**

> *"Begin with your brain and draft with your brain first."*
> — [[anthony-coman]] (Instructional Associate Professor & Writing Coordinator, UF Warrington) — for relational messages; AI for *polish*, not starting point.

> *"Co-piloting is the best approach. Enhance the efficiency and efficacy of the work, but always have a human in the center."*
> — [[linjuan-rita-men]] (Professor of PR, University of Florida)

**Practitioner-side quote (from one of Men's 20 interviews):**

> *"If my manager has to use AI to write a message like this, why are they my manager at all?"*

**Solution framing the article offers:**
- Use AI for transactional/informative content, not relational
- Adopt human-centered "co-piloting"
- Upskill toward strategic, creative, human-experience work
- Establish clear AI usage policies
- Consider context before disclosing AI use

**Companion data (cite from [[simpplr]] press release / 2026 State of IC, not from HR Reporter):**
- **52%** of IC pros use AI weekly+
- **53%** report "very high or significant" efficiency impact
- **29%** of those closest to AI adoption cite job-security anxiety
- **>33%** of orgs cite AI as reason for planned headcount reduction
- Sample: **n=448 IC pros, 85% U.S. / 15% Canada, 500+ employee orgs, fielded December 2025**

## Contradicts

- **The dominant 2026 vendor narrative** that agentic AI is a productivity multiplier for IC. The academic evidence (Cardon & Coman) and practitioner-sentiment evidence (Men et al.; [[simpplr]] survey) say AI use *hurts* perceived sincerity on relational content. Active contradiction with [[workshop]] (Cici), Staffbase (AI quality layer), [[simpplr]] (Comms AI), Firstup (5-pillar suite), Poppulo (Create/Target/Analyze Agents).
- **The vault's existing AI-in-IC sources** — [[forrester-ai-rewrite-ex-deep-listening-2026-05-15]], [[gartner-people-centric-ai-2026-05-15]], [[workvivo-seer-2026-05-11]] — all lean "AI is transformative." This source introduces the opposing thesis: practitioners are *anxious about losing their jobs to AI*, and that anxiety is now a procurement frame. Flagged in [[ai-in-internal-comms]] Tensions.
