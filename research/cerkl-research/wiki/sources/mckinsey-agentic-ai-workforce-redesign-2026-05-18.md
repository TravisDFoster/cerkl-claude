# McKinsey: Redesigning Technology Workforce for the Agentic AI Era

**Author/Publisher:** [[mckinsey]] Technology practice (specific authors unverified due to direct-fetch blocking)  |  **Published:** 2026-04-06  |  **Source type:** Analyst insight piece  |  **URL:** https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-technology/our-insights/designing-an-end-to-end-technology-workforce-for-the-ai-first-era

## Summary

[[mckinsey]]'s April 6 2026 insight piece argues that as agentic AI takes on lower-level engineering work, CIOs must redesign the technology workforce across three pillars — **how talent is hired, how internal capabilities are built and sustained, and how vendor relationships are structured and governed** — and warns that none of it works without *"top-down change management to communicate how agents can be coworkers"*. CIO-fronted with a CHRO co-buyer footprint, the piece is genuinely additive to McKinsey's broader 2026 agentic-AI corpus: it's the operational/prescriptive companion to *"The Rise of the Human-AI Workforce"* (which is macro-trend, all-workforce). For Cerkl, the value isn't in the framework itself — it's the load-bearing supporting line: **change-comms is the gate to AI ROI**. This source pairs with [[gartner-people-centric-ai-2026-05-15]] and [[forrester-ai-rewrite-ex-deep-listening-2026-05-15]] as the **analyst trio** that defines the 2026 IC/EX buying lens — captured at [[analyst-trio-comms-as-ai-gate]].

## Key Takeaways

- **Three-pillar framework, not five.** Talent hiring / internal capability building / vendor relationships — wrapped in four mounting pressures (cost, geopolitics, vendor ROI, talent + reskill + human-agent teams).
- **Agents framed as *coworkers, not features*.** The phrase *"how agents can be coworkers"* is load-bearing — and subtly disruptive to every IC competitor pitching an agentic-suite-inside-a-platform (see [[agents-as-coworkers]]).
- **Change-comms explicitly named as the gate to AI ROI.** *"Without a shared understanding of which skills matter most — and top-down change management to communicate how agents can be coworkers — reskilling efforts tend to remain ineffective."*
- **CIO-fronted with CHRO as implicit co-buyer.** Pairs with [[gartner-people-centric-ai-2026-05-15]] (CHRO-fronted) to bracket both sides of the analyst-validated co-buyer pattern.
- **Genuinely additive to "Rise of the Human-AI Workforce"** — narrowly. *Redesigning* is scoped to the technology function; *Rise* is all-workforce. Cite *Redesigning* for the operational/co-buyer pitch; *Rise* for macro content.
- **The "agents as coworkers" frame may not stick as buzzword** — less clean than Forrester's "deep listening" or Gartner's "enablement illusion." Less likely to become category-defining language; more likely to live inside McKinsey's broader agentic-organization narrative.

## Notable Claims

**The four mounting pressures on technology leaders:**
1. Cut overall costs while delivering more innovation
2. Scale global delivery while managing geopolitical risk
3. Ensure vendor partnerships deliver ROI *"rather than empty promises"*
4. Hire new talent while reskilling existing workforces — *"helping every employee become a technologist"* — while empowering **human–agent teams** to deliver AI at scale

**The three strategic choices:**

- **How technology talent is hired.** *"The central question is no longer how to scale engineering capacity but how to allocate human talent where judgment and decision-making still matter."* War for senior AI talent intensifies *while* agentic AI generates layoffs at the lower-skill end.
- **How internal capabilities are built and sustained.** Reskilling reframed as **change-management problem, not training program**. Load-bearing quote: *"Without a shared understanding of which skills matter most — and top-down change management to communicate how agents can be coworkers — reskilling efforts tend to remain ineffective."*
- **How vendor relationships are structured and governed.** Codeveloping end-to-end with cloud providers and implementation partners — aligning on architecture, governance, risk from the outset — reframes vendor management from procurement to capability-codevelopment.

**The agentic AI workforce framing:** Agents treated as **coworkers, not features**. Hybrid human-agent workforce is the operating model. Successful companies *"infuse agentic AI throughout their organizations… with effective change management to ensure people embrace this shift."*

**Implicit prescriptions to comms / change-management / HR leaders:**
- Top-down change management for the "agent as coworker" reframe
- Shared organizational understanding of which skills matter
- Modeling adoption from the top (per companion *Redefine AI upskilling as a change imperative*: *"Employees will take their cues from how leaders talk about and use AI in their own work."*)
- Active employee participation, not passive consumption (per companion *Reconfiguring work*: *"…ask employees to experiment, cocreate products, and commit to continual skill development."*)

## Cerkl Positioning Note

Two distinct sales angles:

**Buyer-pain angle (Omni AI):** When AEs hear *"we're rolling out [Copilot / Cursor / Devin / an agentic platform] across engineering this year,"* that is the McKinsey-named trigger. Ask: *"Who's running the comms for that — and how do you know it's landing with the engineers, the BAs, and the ops folks who all need different messages?"* Discovery question is now analyst-validated by McKinsey + Gartner + Forrester.

**Counter-positioning angle:** Cerkl's "delivery + measurement layer" thesis fits McKinsey's *agents-as-coworkers* frame in a way that platform-as-agent-container pitches do not. Cerkl can ride the wave by being the **measurement of how AI-narrative comms actually land** — not the source of agentic capability, the substrate that proves change-comms reaches the right employee on the right channel and gets read. Risk to watch: if Firstup or Staffbase repositions ahead of Cerkl as the "agentic-comms-coworker" platform, they capture the McKinsey frame first. Track competitor messaging through Q3 2026.
