# Qualtrics: Employees Experiencing More Change Are MORE Engaged

**Author/Publisher:** Cerkl Internal Research (deepdive) | **Published:** 2026-05-21 | **Source type:** ic-trends deepdive synthesis | **Underlying primary source:** Qualtrics 2026 Global EX Trends Report (press release 2026-02-05)

## Summary
Synthesis deepdive built off a Ragan article (2026-05-21) that surfaced Qualtrics' 2026 Global EX Trends Report finding — employees experiencing significant organizational change report *higher* engagement than those with minimal change. The finding is real (n=33,831, 24 countries) but **conditional on change type, listening cadence, and saturation threshold**. The deepdive triangulates the Qualtrics frame against counter-research from Gallup, Gallagher, Perceptyx, Prosci, and McKinsey — the IC/EX community is split-to-skeptical, with Gallagher's State of the Sector 2026 naming change fatigue as the **#2 IC barrier** (first time on top-five list).

## Key Takeaways
- **The headline finding is real but conditional.** 72% of employees experienced significant org change in the prior 12 months and those reporting change show higher composite engagement than those reporting none. But effect inverts by change type: **investment-coded change (new tech +10pt) lifts engagement; disruptive change (layoffs −7pt) depresses it.**
- **Qualtrics names listening frequency as the moderator — not comms quality.** Orgs that increased listening cadence saw 87% engagement vs 44% (up to 4x EX-metric lift). [[ragan-communications]] added the "comms quality" framing on top in [[sean-devlin]]'s Ragan piece — the distinction matters for citation.
- **Counter-data is substantial.** Gallup's State of the Global Workplace 2026 (engagement at 20%, manager engagement collapsing 27%→22%) argues manager capacity gates the chain. Gallagher's State of the Sector 2026 ranks change fatigue as #2 IC barrier with 61% of orgs lacking a formal change-comms strategy. Perceptyx finds "change management effectiveness" + "confidence in senior leadership" displaced "belonging" as top engagement drivers. Prosci's saturation model says the relationship inverts above a cumulative-change threshold.
- **The "Goldilocks zone" is Granger's hedge.** [[benjamin-granger]] (Chief Workplace Psychologist, Qualtrics): *"The challenge for 2026 is to find the sweet spot of enough pressure to make work engaging and fulfilling, and enough change to keep things fresh and interesting, but not so much that they stress, burn out or alienate your people."*
- **Methodology gap.** Self-reported significant change + composite engagement index. Doesn't isolate which moderator (listening cadence, comms quality, manager capacity, leadership credibility) actually drives the lift.

## Notable Claims
- **72%** of employees experienced significant org change in the prior 12 months (Qualtrics 2026, via [PRNewswire 2026-02-05](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/qualtrics-employees-thrive-through-change-302679648.html)).
- Engagement % by change category: new tech 78%, new policies 77%, workspace changes 76%, strategic shifts 76% (investment-coded); leadership change 67%, manager change 67%, reorganization 66%, downsizing/layoffs 61% (disruptive). Directional gap: **new tech = +10pt; layoffs = −7pt** (same source).
- **Listening-cadence engagement gap: 87% vs 44%** — orgs that increased listening vs those that listened less; up to **4x** EX metric lift. **42%** of employees want *more* frequent listening. (Qualtrics press release.)
- Ragan's 74%/59% specific engagement gap **was not directly verifiable** in the public Qualtrics press release — likely from the gated full ebook. ([Ragan, 2026-05-21](https://www.ragan.com/employees-experiencing-more-change-are-more-engaged/))
- Gallagher State of the Sector 2026: change fatigue = **#2 IC barrier** (first appearance on top-five); **61%** of orgs have no formal change-comms strategy; over-communicating makes employees **30% more likely to lose trust in leadership** and 24% more burned out.
- Perceptyx 2026 EX Trends: "Change management has become the single strongest predictor of employee engagement" and "change fatigue doesn't discriminate between positive and negative change" — perceptions of change handling fell two consecutive years.
- Abhijit Bhaduri (HR analyst, 2026-05-14): *"Persistently low turnover in this environment is often misread as evidence of effective change management, when it more accurately reflects constrained mobility."*
- Helen Baldwin (IC practitioner, LinkedIn): change fatigue *"has just shot to number two on the list of biggest challenges facing internal communications professionals"* — employees absorb ~50% of changes being implemented.

## Contradicts
- Headline contradicts the dominant 2025–2026 "change fatigue is corroding engagement" frame held by [[gallagher]], [[perceptyx]], and the practitioner LinkedIn discourse. The Qualtrics frame **only** holds for investment-coded change; for layoffs/leadership turnover the relationship inverts (−7pt) and aligns with the fatigue thesis.
- The Ragan summary attributes the moderator to "comms quality"; the primary Qualtrics materials name *listening frequency* specifically. Practitioner citations need to preserve the distinction.
