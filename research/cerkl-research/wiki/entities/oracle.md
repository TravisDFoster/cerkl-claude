# Oracle

**Type:** Fortune 100 enterprise software / cloud vendor (org)  |  **Founded:** 1977  |  **Workforce (pre-cut):** ~162,000 globally  |  **Website:** https://www.oracle.com

## Overview

Multinational technology corporation; database / ERP / cloud-infrastructure vendor. Enters this vault in May 2026 as the **highest-scale anchor case in the 2026 [[layoff-comms-choreography]] reference set** — Oracle's 2026-03-31 mass-email layoff (~20,000–30,000 employees, ~18% of global headcount) is now the cleanest single-incident illustration of *delivery-as-comms-strategy* failure in the public record.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **The 6 a.m. mass-email layoff (2026-03-31):** single email, signed "Oracle Leadership" (unnamed), system access revoked within minutes, no manager cascade, no leaver/stayer segmentation, no follow-up — three sentences and grammatically fine, choreography catastrophic.
- **AI-capex driver, not performance.** Bloomberg/CNBC: ~$8–10B annual cash flow redirected to AI data-center buildout; record revenue and backlog at the time of cuts.
- **India hardest hit:** ~12,000 (~40% of Oracle India).
- **Severance backlash:** 600+ Oracle employees signed open letter requesting benchmark-matched severance ([TechCrunch 2026-05-08](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/08/laid-off-oracle-workers-tried-to-negotiate-better-severance-oracle-said-no/)); Oracle declined to negotiate. Severance: 4 weeks + 1 week/year tenure, capped at 26 weeks.
- **Slack telemetry signal:** Active users dropped ~165,000 → ~155,000 in hours post-email.
