# Boston Consulting Group (BCG)

**Type:** Management consulting / analyst firm  |  **Founded:** 1963  |  **Website:** https://www.bcg.com

## Overview

BCG is one of the dominant management-consulting voices on AI transformation in 2026, with a distinct angle: behavioral-design as the operating system for change. Where [[mckinsey]]'s *Rewired 2.0* frames transformation as a talent-and-comms infrastructure problem, BCG (led by behavioral-science partner [[julia-dhar]]) goes deeper than comms — treating comms as a surface artifact of a deeper *participation* problem.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **"AI Transformation Is a Workforce Transformation"** (2026): explicitly names that failed AI transformations fail on human factors; top-performing companies pair AI with the most ambitious upskilling.
- **Behavioral-design as the lever, not just comms.** BCG's frame treats comms as a downstream artifact of how change is co-created with employees.
- Julia Dhar is the lead behavioral-science voice; her work on change resistance and behavior change is widely cited in HBR.
