# McKinsey & Company

**Type:** Management consulting / analyst firm  |  **Founded:** 1926  |  **Relevant practices:** McKinsey Technology, McKinsey Global Institute, People & Organizational Performance, QuantumBlack  |  **Website:** https://www.mckinsey.com

## Overview

Global management consulting firm; one of the dominant CIO/CHRO-level advisory voices on the agentic-AI workforce reshape. Distinct from [[gartner]] / [[forrester]] in being primarily *consulting-derived* (client engagements) rather than survey/research-derived; its 2026 agentic-AI corpus is unusually large (Redesigning Technology Workforce, Rise of the Human-AI Workforce, Reconfiguring Work, Agents Robots and Us, Superagency in the Workplace).

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **The "agents as coworkers, not features" frame** — load-bearing across the McKinsey 2026 corpus; [[agents-as-coworkers]] concept entry derives from it.
- **Change-comms named as the gate to AI ROI** — *"Without top-down change management to communicate how agents can be coworkers, reskilling efforts tend to remain ineffective"* (Redesigning Technology Workforce, 2026-04-06).
- **The CIO-CHRO co-buyer pattern** — Redesigning is CIO-fronted; Rise of the Human-AI Workforce is CHRO-fronted; together they bracket both sides.
- **Three-pillar workforce-redesign framework:** talent hiring / internal capability building / vendor relationships, wrapped in four pressures (cost, geopolitics, vendor ROI, talent + reskill + human-agent teams).
