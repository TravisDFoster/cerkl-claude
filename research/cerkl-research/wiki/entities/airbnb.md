# Airbnb

**Type:** Travel-marketplace platform  |  **Founded:** 2008  |  **Website:** https://news.airbnb.com

## Overview

Airbnb is the gold-standard reference point for layoff-comms craft in the 2020-2026 era, anchored by [[brian-chesky]]'s 2020-05-05 memo. Six years on, the memo is still cited verbatim in HBR, SHRM, and IC trade-press playbooks — its specifics (14 weeks base severance + tenure bonus, 12 months COBRA, equity cliff waiver, laptop keep, alumni placement directory) defined a generation of what "humane layoff comms" looks like.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **Chesky's 2020 memo:** 3,374 words, led with the financial "why," named the specific teams affected, then detailed concrete severance — pairing transparency with material support, not language alone.
- **The alumni placement directory** is a structural innovation — externalizing the firm's responsibility to outplaced employees by publishing them as a public, opt-in resource for other companies hiring.
- The "led with the financial why" pattern is the explicit model that [[chuck-robbins]] / [[cisco]] cited in 2026 — but with a 6-year-later wrinkle: Cisco led with *positive* financials (record quarter) rather than Chesky's *negative* (COVID-driven revenue collapse), which materially changes how the framing lands.
