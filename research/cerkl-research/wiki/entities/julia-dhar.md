# Julia Dhar

**Type:** Person  |  **Role:** Partner and Managing Director, [[bcg]] (Behavioral Science lead)  |  **Website:** https://www.bcg.com/about/people/experts/julia-dhar

## Overview

Julia Dhar is BCG's behavioral-science lead and a primary voice on the human-factors angle of AI transformation. Frequently cited in HBR and BCG's flagship publications on AI workforce change. Her framing — change failure as a behavior problem rather than a strategy problem — is the most articulated counterpoint to McKinsey's program-mechanics framing in *Rewired 2.0*.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **Change failure as behavior, not strategy** — *"leaders over-design and under-communicate motivation"* (HBR 2026 management tips on organizational change, citing Dhar's work).
- Co-author of BCG's 2026 *"AI Transformation Is a Workforce Transformation"* publication framing.
- Public-debate background — World Debate Champion — informs the framing of change comms as co-created with employees, not delivered to them.
