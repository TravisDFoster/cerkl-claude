# Robert Sutton

**Type:** Person  |  **Role:** Professor of Management Science and Engineering, Stanford  |  **Website:** https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/faculty/robert-sutton

## Overview

Robert (Bob) Sutton is a Stanford organizational-behavior scholar whose work on "the no asshole rule," "scaling up excellence," and difficult-conversation comms is widely cited in HBR. His [[pucc-framework]] — Predictability, Understanding, Control, Compassion — is the through-line HBR is using in 2025-2026 updates on layoff comms, and the underlying logic anchoring why the 2026 AI-restructure subgenre's "good" templates land and the "bad" ones don't.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **[[pucc-framework]]:** Predictability, Understanding, Control, Compassion. Minimize trauma by telling people what's coming, why, what choices they have, and showing you see their pain.
- Argues that managers who botch difficult conversations cost more in long-term trust and team performance than the short-term cost of the decision being communicated.
- 2025 HBR Q&A on communicating difficult decisions is the canonical 2025 reference: https://hbr.org/2025/07/qa-professor-robert-sutton-on-communicating-difficult-decisions-as-a-leader
