# IC Deep Dive — Qualtrics "More Change = More Engaged" finding

**Date:** 2026-05-21   |   **Category:** Trend (research finding)

---

## TL;DR
Qualtrics' 2026 Global EX Trends Report (33,831 employees, 24 countries) claims employees experiencing significant org change are *more* engaged than those who aren't — but only conditional on (a) change type, (b) listening cadence, and (c) staying under saturation. The IC/EX community is split-to-skeptical: Gallagher, Perceptyx, McKinsey and Prosci all have data saying the relationship inverts above a threshold or breaks without manager capacity. The headline finding is real, but the moderators are load-bearing — and the moderator Qualtrics names is *listening frequency*, not the broader "comms quality" frame Ragan put on it.

## What it is
Research finding from **Qualtrics' 2026 Global Employee Experience Trends Report**, lead spokesperson **Dr. Benjamin Granger (Chief Workplace Psychologist)**, surfaced into IC trade press via [Ragan, 2026-05-21](https://www.ragan.com/employees-experiencing-more-change-are-more-engaged/). Original press release [2026-02-05](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/qualtrics-employees-thrive-through-change-302679648.html). Methodology: 33,831 full- and part-time employees, 24 countries, 5 continents, 30 industries, orgs 100 → 50,000+, fieldwork Sept–Oct 2025.

**Source URLs:**

## What it says — concretely
- **72%** of employees experienced significant org change in the prior 12 months; those who did report *higher* engagement than those with minimal change.
- The effect is **conditional on change type** (engagement % by change category):
  - **Investment / forward-looking** (positive driver): new tech 78%, new policies 77%, workspace changes 76%, strategic shifts 76%
  - **Disruptive** (negative driver): leadership change 67%, manager change 67%, reorganization 66%, downsizing/layoffs 61%
  - Directional gap: **new tech = +10 pt engagement; layoffs = −7 pt engagement**
- **Granger's "Goldilocks zone" verbatim:** *"The challenge for 2026 is to find the sweet spot of enough pressure to make work engaging and fulfilling, and enough change to keep things fresh and interesting, but not so much that they stress, burn out or alienate your people."*
- **The named moderator is listening frequency, not comms quality.** Orgs that increased listening saw engagement of **87% vs. 44%** for those that listened less — up to **4x higher** EX metrics. **42%** of employees want *more* frequent listening.
- Adjacent stats worth holding: 50% of employees use AI frequently, only 20% stick to company-approved tools (shadow-AI risk); new-hire engagement down 7 pts YoY; frontline 65% (−3); part-time 63% (−7).
- **Ragan's specific 74%/59% gap was not directly verifiable in the public press release** — likely pulled from the gated full ebook. Cite Qualtrics' breakdown by change type rather than the Ragan headline number if precision matters.

## Who it's for / when it's useful
- **Primary use:** IC, EX, and HR leaders running large change programs (RTO, reorgs, AI rollouts, M&A). The frame is a useful counter-narrative to the dominant "change fatigue" pitch most vendors are using.
- **Strong fit when:** the change is *investment-coded* (new tech, new policies, strategic shift) and you can pair the program with elevated listening cadence (pulse surveys, manager 1:1 prompts, town halls with Q&A).
- **Weak fit when:** the change is layoff/leadership/manager turnover, manager capacity is already collapsed, or cumulative change is past saturation. In those cases the Gallagher/Perceptyx/Prosci data is more honest.

## Gotchas
- **The headline is conditional, not universal.** Engagement only climbs with *investment-coded* change. For layoffs and leadership churn, the relationship inverts and the gap is −7 pts. Anyone quoting the headline without that breakdown is misusing the data.
- **Qualtrics names listening, not comms.** The press materials emphasize listening frequency as the moderator, not comms quality. Ragan added the "comms quality" framing on top. If you cite Qualtrics directly in marketing copy, lead with listening; if you cite Ragan, lead with comms — but don't confuse the two.
- **Granger's "Goldilocks" caveat is the loudest hedge in the press release.** Use it. Skipping the saturation acknowledgment is what's drawing the practitioner pushback.
- **The 74%/59% Ragan figure isn't in the public Qualtrics release** — verify in the gated ebook before quoting in customer-facing material.
- **Methodology limitation:** self-reported significant change + standard composite engagement index. Doesn't isolate which moderator (listening vs. comms quality vs. manager capacity vs. leadership credibility) is actually driving the lift — and Gallup/Perceptyx/Gallagher all argue manager capacity is the silent variable.
