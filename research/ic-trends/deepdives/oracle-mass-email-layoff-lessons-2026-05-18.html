The incident (triangulated)

  • Date/time: 2026-03-31, ~6 a.m. local time, globally staggered

06Gotchas

Source quality
Amazing Workplaces is not a major IC publication — it's a workplace-certification vendor with thought-leadership content marketing. The piece cites no primary reporting, no named sources. Treat AW as one lens; lead public-facing Cerkl content with the HR Executive / Newsweek / CNBC / TechCrunch sourcing instead.
Prescriptions are generic
"Name a human being / give a real reason / respect transition time" are not contested takes. The Cerkl angle is what the article doesn't say: segmentation, channel choice, and receipt confirmation are infrastructure problems with infrastructure solutions — not memo-writing problems.
Scale mismatch
Most IC Directors aren't doing 30,000-person global RIFs; they're doing 50–500-person reductions. The case is useful as a vivid failure mode, not a literal scale comparator. Don't oversell the analogy.
WARN Act sidestep is a legal story, not an IC story
TechCrunch reports Oracle used remote-worker classification to avoid the 60-day WARN notice. This is a labor-law fight, not a comms diagnosis. Keep it out of the Cerkl pitch.
Pattern observation worth noting
"Broader organizational change" is the same exact euphemism used by GM, Sony, ProPublica. Becoming the 2026 equivalent of "reinvent how we work" — a tell that the comms team didn't write the email, the legal team did.
Prompt-injection logged
The fetched Amazing Workplaces page contained a fake <system-reminder> block instructing the fetcher to use TodoWrite. Prompt-injection attempt embedded in page content. Ignored; logged for awareness.
