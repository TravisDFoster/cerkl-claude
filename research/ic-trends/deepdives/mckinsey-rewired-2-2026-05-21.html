McKinsey “Rewired 2.0: The Talent Plan”

01What it is & what it says

What it is

Blog post + companion book launch. McKinsey Senior Partners Eric Lamarre, Kate Smaje, and Rob Levin (with book co-authors Alex Singla and Alexander Sukharevsky) published “Rewired 2.0: The Talent Plan” on the McKinsey Organization Blog on 2026-05-04, anchoring the 2nd-edition launch of Rewired (Wiley, ISBN 9781394381906).

Coordinated launch wave: McKinsey Author Talks interview, the McKinsey Podcast, a McKinsey Live webinar (“Rewired to win”), and the standalone “AI Transformation Manifesto” (2026-04-07).

What’s new vs. Rewired 1.0 (2023)

  • Adds Levin/Singla/Sukharevsky; drops Zemmel
  • Subtitle shifts from “Outcompeting in the Age of Digital and AI” to “How Leading Companies Win with Technology and AI”
  • 30+ new case studies
  • Adds the two “70–30 flips” (in-house and doer-engineer)
  • Adds the agentic workforce design (people + agents + automation)
  • Hard claim: AI-rewired companies lift EBITDA by ~20%

The four levers leaders underweight

LEVER 01

Tech muscle of business leaders

Targeted dev journeys for the N-2 / N-3 layer — domain owners who can build an AI-enabled road map and assess the health of their data, platforms, and engineering talent.

LEVER 02

Transform the talent model

Two 70–30 flips: >70% of tech in-house, >70% of IT as doer-engineers. Workforce planning budgets three classes of capacity — people, agents, automation.

LEVER 03

Innovation-friendly workplace

Recognition and learning journeys for technical talent, “from the first interview through to meaningful learning journeys.” Bureaucracy is the named blocker.

LEVER 04

Human–agentic collaboration

Managers shift from supervising tasks to orchestrating hybrid systems — guardrails, handoffs, judgment at the edge, and the emotional work of helping humans feel safe alongside machine colleagues. The IC hook.

02Who it’s for / when it’s useful

Primary use

CXO-level decision frame for IC, EX, HR, and digital transformation leaders. Highest utility for the C-suite stakeholder conversation, not the practitioner workbench.

Strong fit when

The org is mid-AI-transformation and you need analyst air cover for the argument that transformation infrastructure (comms cadence, manager enablement, listening loops, role redesign) is the binding constraint — not the tooling. Lever #4 is the strongest IC hook.

Weak fit when

The buyer wants a tactical IC playbook (Rewired 2.0 isn’t one), or when McKinsey-as-vendor credibility is contested — which, given the simultaneous McKinsey layoffs, is a real and growing dynamic.

05Gotchas

The McKinsey hypocrisy critique

McKinsey is cutting ~10% of non-client-facing staff (~5,000 roles since 2023) while publishing a talent playbook. The dominant cynicism online frames the book as “a productivity story for partners, and a headcount story for everyone below them.” Anyone citing the work to a sophisticated buyer needs to acknowledge this — it’s the credibility test.

The four “levers” are not labeled as a comms list

McKinsey calls them levers/unlocks; the comms angle has to be drawn out (especially via Lever #4). Be honest about that framing if quoting verbatim.

The 20% EBITDA lift claim

Drawn from McKinsey’s own case-collection methodology — defensible at analyst grade, less so under independent statistical scrutiny. Cite carefully.
