Qualtrics: "Employees Experiencing More Change Are More Engaged"

Date: 2026-05-21 Category: Trend — research finding Source: Qualtrics 2026 Global EX Trends Report Borrow with caveats — Maybe

Qualtrics' 2026 Global EX Trends Report (33,831 employees, 24 countries) claims employees experiencing significant org change are more engaged than those who aren't — but only conditional on (a) change type, (b) listening cadence, and (c) staying under saturation. Gallagher, Perceptyx, McKinsey and Prosci all have data saying the relationship inverts above threshold or breaks without manager capacity. The headline is real; the moderators are load-bearing — and the moderator Qualtrics names is listening frequency, not the broader "comms quality" frame Ragan put on it.

02Who it's for / when it's useful

Primary user

IC, EX, and HR leaders running large change programs (RTO, reorgs, AI rollouts, M&A). Useful counter-narrative to the dominant "change fatigue" pitch most vendors use.

Strong fit when

The change is investment-coded (new tech, new policies, strategic shift) and the program is paired with elevated listening cadence (pulse surveys, manager 1:1 prompts, town halls with Q&A).

Weak fit when

The change is layoff / leadership / manager turnover, manager capacity is collapsed, or cumulative change is past saturation. Use Gallagher/Perceptyx/Prosci data instead.

05Gotchas

The headline is conditional, not universal
Engagement only climbs with investment-coded change. For layoffs and leadership churn the relationship inverts and the gap is −7 pts. Anyone quoting the headline without that breakdown is misusing the data.
Qualtrics names listening, not comms
The press materials emphasize listening frequency as the moderator, not comms quality. Ragan added the "comms quality" framing on top. If you cite Qualtrics directly in marketing copy, lead with listening; if you cite Ragan, lead with comms — but don't confuse the two.
Goldilocks caveat is the loudest hedge
Granger's "sweet spot" line is the saturation acknowledgment in the press release. Skipping it is what's drawing the practitioner pushback. Use it.
74% / 59% Ragan figure not in public release
Verify in the gated ebook before quoting in customer-facing material.
Methodology limitation
Self-reported significant change + standard composite engagement index. Doesn't isolate which moderator (listening vs. comms quality vs. manager capacity vs. leadership credibility) is actually driving the lift — and Gallup/Perceptyx/Gallagher all argue manager capacity is the silent variable.
