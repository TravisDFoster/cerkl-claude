# IC Deep Dive — McKinsey "Rewired 2.0: The Talent Plan"

**Date:** 2026-05-21   |   **Category:** Analyst report

---

## TL;DR
McKinsey's 2026-05-04 Organization Blog post (Lamarre/Smaje/Levin) launches the 2nd-edition *Rewired* book with the thesis: *"At the heart of every digital transformation is a talent transformation."* The piece argues leaders systematically underweight four people-and-AI levers — building the AI muscle of N-2/N-3 business leaders, redesigning the talent model around people + agents + automation, fixing the workplace for tech talent, and preparing managers for human-agentic collaboration. The four levers are the cleanest analyst air cover for the position that AI rollouts fail on comms infrastructure, not tooling — but the loudest practitioner critique is McKinsey publishing a talent playbook while cutting ~10% of its own non-client staff.

## What it is
**Blog post + companion book launch.** McKinsey Senior Partners **Eric Lamarre, Kate Smaje, and Rob Levin** (with book co-authors **Alex Singla** and **Alexander Sukharevsky**) published "Rewired 2.0: The Talent Plan" on the McKinsey Organization Blog on **2026-05-04**, anchoring the launch of the 2nd edition of *Rewired* (Wiley, ISBN 9781394381906). The post is part of a coordinated launch wave including a McKinsey Author Talks interview, the McKinsey Podcast, a McKinsey Live webinar ("Rewired to win"), and the standalone "AI Transformation Manifesto" (2026-04-07).

## What it says — concretely
The 2.0 thesis is operationalized through **four levers** that leaders systematically underweight relative to the tech stack:

1. **Build the tech muscle of business leaders.** Specifically the **N-2 and N-3 layer** (two and three levels below the CEO) — domain owners who can "develop an AI-enabled transformation road map, understand modern software delivery, and appreciate the health of their data estate, technology platforms, and engineering talent." Remedy: targeted dev journeys, not just generic training.
2. **Transform the talent model.** Two "70–30 flips": **>70% of tech talent in-house** (vs. outsourced) and **>70% of the IT cohort as doer-engineers** (vs. managers). Workforce planning now budgets **three classes of capacity** — people, agents, and (where relevant) physical automation — each with different reliability and governance profiles.
3. **Create an innovation-friendly workplace for tech talent.** Recognition and learning journeys for technical talent, "from the first interview through to the construction of meaningful learning journeys." Bureaucracy is the named blocker.
4. **Prepare for human–agentic collaboration.** Managers shift "from supervising tasks to orchestrating hybrid systems — guardrails, handoffs, and judgment at the edge, along with **the emotional work of helping humans feel safe alongside machine colleagues**." This is the comms-adjacent lever — explicit naming of the emotional / change-comms dimension.

**What's new vs. Rewired 1.0 (2023):** Adds Levin/Singla/Sukharevsky (drops Zemmel); subtitle shifts from "Outcompeting in the Age of Digital and AI" to "How Leading Companies Win with Technology and AI"; adds 30+ new case studies, the 70–30 flips, the agentic workforce design, and a hard claim that AI-rewired companies lift EBITDA by **~20%**.

## Who it's for / when it's useful
- **Primary use:** CXO-level decision frame for IC, EX, HR, and digital transformation leaders. Highest utility for the C-suite stakeholder conversation, not the practitioner workbench.
- **Strong fit when:** the org is mid-AI-transformation and you need analyst air cover for the argument that *transformation infrastructure* (comms cadence, manager enablement, listening loops, role redesign) is the binding constraint — not the tooling. Lever #4 (human-agentic) is the strongest IC hook.
- **Weak fit when:** the buyer wants a tactical IC playbook (it's not one), or when the McKinsey-as-vendor credibility is contested (which, given the simultaneous McKinsey layoffs, is a real and growing dynamic).

## Gotchas
- **The McKinsey hypocrisy critique is the loudest practitioner reaction online.** McKinsey is cutting ~10% of non-client-facing staff (~5,000 roles since 2023) while publishing a talent playbook. The dominant cynicism online frames the book as "a productivity story for partners, and a headcount story for everyone below them." Anyone citing the work to a sophisticated buyer needs to acknowledge this — it's the credibility test.
- **The four "levers" are not labeled as a comms list.** McKinsey calls them levers/unlocks; the comms angle has to be drawn out (especially via Lever #4). Be honest about that framing if quoting verbatim.
- **The 20% EBITDA lift claim is the headline number to be careful with.** It's drawn from McKinsey's own case-collection methodology — defensible at the level of analyst grade, less so under independent statistical scrutiny.
- **Direct WebFetch of the McKinsey blog post is rate-limited.** Quotes in this deep-dive are surfaced via search snippets; verbatim phrasing should be re-checked against the live page before publishing customer-facing copy.
