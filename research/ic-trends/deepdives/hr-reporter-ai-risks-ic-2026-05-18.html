The thesis

AI-assisted internal comms erodes trust and authenticity, especially in relational messages — recognition, congratulations, motivation, performance reviews, crisis comms. The fix is “co-piloting”: humans draft, AI polishes. Not “single-prompt-to-campaign” agentic delivery.

Two studies cited: Cardon & Coman (IJBC 2025, n=1,100 working professionals) on AI-assisted workplace writing; Linjuan Rita Men et al. (Public Relations Review, n=20 senior comms pros) on AI in IC.

Risks the article enumerates

  • Trust erosion — recipients perceive AI-written messages as less authentic when AI assistance is high.
  • Authenticity loss — “lack of human experience and human touch in the communication.”
  • Reputational harm to the sender — using AI may damage supervisor credibility and perceived caring.
  • Skill atrophy — over-reliance on AI tools without the expected time savings.
  • Wrong tool for high-stakes scenarios — performance reviews and crisis comms specifically called out.
  • Sincerity decline for relational content — congratulations, motivation, recognition all degraded by visible AI use.

06Gotchas

The 52% stat is not in the HR Reporter article

It's from Simpplr, fielded the same month the article published. The parent brief conflated the two; they're complementary signals from different sources, not the same source.

HR Reporter audience skew

Canadian HR Reporter is HR-led, not pure IC. The piece carries less weight with a Senior Director of IC at a Fortune 500 than it would for a People Ops / HR generalist. Cite alongside Simpplr for IC-specific audiences.

Academic citations are paywalled

Both Cardon & Coman (IJBC 2025) and Men et al. (Public Relations Review) sit behind academic paywalls. Cerkl marketing can cite abstracts and HR Reporter's summary; long-form Cerkular content would need a one-off academic-access pull.

“Co-piloting” is already loaded vocabulary

Microsoft owns “Copilot” as a product name. Use “human-in-the-loop” or “human-centered AI” in Cerkl copy to avoid trademark adjacency and signal independence from the Microsoft framing.

Simpplr is both source and target

Simpplr is the source of the anxiety data and a maximalist AI vendor. Cerkl can quote Simpplr's own data back at Simpplr's own marketing — but do so once, sharply, not as a sustained campaign (looks petty over time).
