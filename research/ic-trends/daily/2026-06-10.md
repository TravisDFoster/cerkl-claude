# IC Trends — 2026-06-10

**Today's headline takeaway:** Two analyst moves are quietly hostile to the governance-led pitch your top competitors are now all running. Forrester turned EX into a scored category (Total Experience Score with an EX Index baked in), and Gartner published a take that says uniform AI-agent governance is itself a failure mode — both widen the lane for "we market what we measure" as the counter-narrative.

## Top items

### 1. Forrester launches the EX Index (inside a new "Total Experience Score" framework) — EX is now graded like CX
**Source:** Forrester   |   **URL:** https://www.forrester.com/blogs/forresters-ex-index-completes-the-total-experience-picture/   |   **Date:** 2026-06-08   |   **Category:** Analyst
Forrester unveiled the EX Index — a measurable score across empowerment, enablement, inspiration — at CX Summit EMEA. 37% of 150 US brands score *negative* on EX; only 25% positive. EX is now a graded, scored, indexed category alongside CX.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Analyst formalization of EX measurement is upstream of every IC vendor's roadmap. Cerkl's "delivery + measurement layer" frame gets cleaner air the more EX moves toward scored-and-benchmarked. Watch which vendors latch onto the EX Index in their next deck — Workvivo (Forrester Wave Leader) is the most likely.

### 2. Gartner: "Uniform AI agent governance will lead to enterprise AI agent failure"
**Source:** Gartner   |   **URL:** https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-05-26-gartner-says-applying-uniform-governance-across-ai-agents-will-lead-to-enterprise-ai-agent-failure   |   **Date:** 2026-05-26   |   **Category:** Analyst
Gartner argues differentiated governance — based on agent autonomy, risk profile, and business context — beats blanket policy. Direct contradiction of the "one-size-fits-all AI governance layer" pitch that Staffbase (Navigator), Simpplr (AI Control Center), and LumApps (Agent Hub) have all converged on since late May.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Reinforces the counter-position drafted in W22 — *"Governance is what you market when you don't trust your AI. We market what we measure."* Use this Gartner pickup as the cited spine of the next vs. Simpplr / vs. Staffbase refresh. Three weeks ago the governance frame was a competitive threat; today it's a category cliché with analyst pushback.
