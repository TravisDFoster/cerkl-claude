# IC Trends — 2026-05-21

**Today's headline takeaway:** The IC narrative on change is flipping — Qualtrics data via Ragan shows employees in heavy-change orgs are *more* engaged, not less. Paired with Cisco's "strong-quarter layoff" memo and Forrester's call that half of AI layoffs will be quietly reversed, the week's signal converges on one message: opaque comms — not change itself — is what corrodes trust.

## Top items

### 1. Ragan / Qualtrics — Employees experiencing more change are *more* engaged
**Source:** Ragan   |   **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/employees-experiencing-more-change-are-more-engaged/   |   **Date:** 2026-05-21   |   **Category:** Publication
Qualtrics' new dataset flips the conventional wisdom: it's instability — not change itself — that drags engagement down. Employees can absorb a lot of change if the comms cadence is steady and the rationale is clear.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** A direct frame Cerkl can use in change-comms positioning and Foundations sales copy. Most competitor "AI/RTO change" pitches assume change = engagement risk; this study reframes the variable to *comms quality*, which is Cerkl's lane.
