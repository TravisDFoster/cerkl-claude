## Pain-point cross-cut

> The buyer problems competitors are leading with this week, derived across positioning + ads + content. Most actionable section for Cerkl's marketing.

### Pain point 1: "Shadow AI" — Staffbase retreated, LumApps held, the frame absorbed into "AI-native" claims

- **Who's claiming it (W24):** LumApps (*"Cut shadow IT, reduce complexity"* still elevated to hero), Simpplr (extended sprint — 2 more posts this week on shadow-AI governance + IC as the AI change-management function).
- **Who softened or pulled back:** **Staffbase** — W23's *"Our employees are already using AI tools we haven't approved"* + *"79%"* shadow-AI hook is **gone from the homepage hero**; replaced by trust-and-reach framing (*"Information employees trust. Everywhere they work."*). Simpplr's hero also retreated from *"AI Deployment … Fragmented Knowledge"* to the tamer *"AI-powered intranet for modern enterprises."*
- **How they're framing it now (verbatim):**
  - LumApps: *"Cut shadow IT, reduce complexity, and give every employee one secure place to work"* ([lumapps.com](https://www.lumapps.com/))
  - Simpplr: *"Three strategies to stop shadow AI without killing productivity"* (sustained from W23) + *"IC owns AI rollout"* POV ([Simpplr 2026-06-08](https://www.simpplr.com/blog/internal-communications-ai-adoption/))
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Cerkl has no shadow-AI positioning today. The W23 recommendation (counter-positioning POV) has not shipped.
- **Implication:** **The pain frame is mutating, not dying.** Vendors are absorbing shadow-AI into their broader *"AI-native"* category claims (5 of 10 vendors now lead with AI-native/AI-powered platform language) — i.e., "we ARE the sanctioned AI" rather than naming the fear in the hook. This is a less actionable surface for Cerkl than W23's stat-led ad hooks. **Recommendation: still ship the counter-positioning POV from W23 — but pivot the angle from "shadow AI is a content problem" to "the sanctioned-AI promise is undermined the moment employees don't read the message."** The fragmenting-AI-message problem is sharper than the unsanctioned-tools problem now.

### Pain point 2: "Fragmentation" / right-message-right-person — still 4-vendor dominant (carries from W22-W23)

- **Who's claiming it:** Simpplr, LumApps, Firstup, Poppulo. Plus Firstup's *"75% miss critical updates"* 2026 IC research ([firstup.io](https://firstup.io/blog/internal-communications-failure-2026-research/)) still anchoring lead-gen.
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Cerkl's positioning (*"delivery + measurement layer across the channels you already have"*) is directly the answer. Cerkl is competing for the *segmentation* phrasing, not the *fragmentation* phrasing — the W23 recommendation to adopt "fragmentation" as a hero word for at least one Foundations+ LP variant remains valid.
- **Implication:** **Lean in — and pair with the W24 measurement-axis emerging from Firstup + AxiosHQ + ContactMonkey.** Three vendors are now converging on a measurement/outcomes hook (*"Don't just communicate. Get outcomes."* Firstup; *"Eliminate guesswork and demonstrate clear internal communications impact."* ContactMonkey). Cerkl's positioning hits both axes natively. Run the W23 fragmentation LP variant against the existing copy, and add a measurement-hero variant alongside.

### Pain point 3: "We launched AI but can't see outcomes" — execution-gap frame still 3 CEOs (W20→W24)

- **Who's claiming it:** Same three from W22-W23 — Cates (ContactMonkey W20), Black (Workvivo W21), Fornell (Poppulo W22). Sharma + Böhringer **still have not adopted** the frame in W24 despite both being active publishers this week.
- **Implication:** **Window persists into week 4 — but Cerkl's "AI writes; Cerkl delivers and proves" is now a stale recommendation if it hasn't shipped.** Recommend collapsing this from "first-mover opportunity" to "shipped now or stop chasing." If the W22 homepage A/B test was not queued by W22 close, the value of originating the frame is decaying weekly. Consider it a "ship-this-week or kill" decision.

## Ideas to steal

- **Conquest SEO defense at full sprint cadence (Staffbase W24 playbook).** Staffbase shipped 3 "X Alternatives" pages in 7 days against its top 3 competitors. This is the new aggressive baseline for IC-category SEO defense.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* The W23 "tit-for-tat conquest listicle preparedness" recommendation needs urgency. Two moves: (1) Defensive — publish *"Cerkl vs. the alternatives — when to pick what"* on the Cerkl blog this sprint, with honest "if you want X, pick Y" sections. Defensive but owns the SERP. (2) Offensive — Workshop is the most direct Foundations head-to-head, and now has a #1 G2 newsletter badge to lean on. *"Best Workshop Alternatives 2026"* is now the highest-leverage offensive listicle on the board, not the cheapest. Pair with *"Best Axios HQ Alternatives 2026"* (per `shared/competitors.md`, cheapest competitive SEO win).

- **Productized trial as AI sub-brand access (ContactMonkey CoAuthor playbook).** Instead of a generic "free trial," brand a specific AI capability as the trial entry point. Keeps the platform trial structurally absent (less revenue cannibalization) while giving the buyer something concrete to play with.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Cerkl Foundations is already free, so the inverse applies — surface the *Omni AI* capability (or a specific personalization feature) as a guided "Try the AI" entry point INSIDE Foundations, with prompts that demonstrate read-rate measurability. Productize the upsell trial inside the free tier, not as a separate signup.

- **G2 category-badge promotion as front-and-center positioning (Workshop W24 playbook).** Workshop's #1 newsletter award is now the bottom-funnel objection-handler for every Workshop deal. Cerkl needs to know which G2 categories it could credibly win — and lean into the badge as positioning.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Audit Cerkl's current G2 standing in the IC + intranet + newsletter categories. Identify the 1-2 categories Cerkl could win for Fall 2026 with focused review-velocity push (offer Foundations users a "leave a G2 review" prompt in-product). Pair with a Q4 G2 badge campaign. Workshop's example shows the badge becomes hero-headline material, not a footer trust signal.

- **Customer-stories pivot to mid-market manufacturing/APAC (Staffbase W24 playbook).** Staffbase's customer-stories page dethroned the W23 F500 marquee logos (DHL, PGA Tour, Adidas, Alaska Air) and now leads with mid-market manufacturing + APAC gaming (Cladtek 2K, Tabcorp 3K, YAGEO 40K, Toyota Finance AU). That's the same 2K–10K segment Cerkl targets.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Surface Cerkl's strongest 2K–10K manufacturing/distribution logos at the top of the /customers page (currently buried below F500 logos). Match Staffbase's segment-of-emphasis pivot one quarter behind, while the territory is contested.

- **Universal complaint = customization/template ceilings — durable Cerkl wedge.** Across Staffbase, Simpplr, LumApps, Poppulo, AxiosHQ, ContactMonkey reviews, the same complaint surfaces: customization walls and template limitations.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* The W23 stat-led ad-headline idea applies directly: lead one Foundations LP variant with *"You don't need to redesign your channels — Cerkl personalizes the message inside the channels you already have"* against a backdrop of competitor template-customization complaints (quoted G2 fragments, attributed honestly). One-screen LP, single-stat hero.
