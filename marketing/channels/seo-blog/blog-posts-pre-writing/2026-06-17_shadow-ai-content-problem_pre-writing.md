## Outline

**H1:** Shadow AI Is a Content Problem (Not a Governance Problem)

**Intro (3–4 short paragraphs):** Lead with the observation that makes the thesis land — three of the biggest names in IC software are now spending ad impressions on the same five words ("our employees are using AI"). All three are pitching governance. The buyer they're describing is IT. The buyer who actually owns this problem is internal comms — and IC doesn't shop for control panels.

**H2 — Why employees go to ChatGPT for the answer that should have come from you**

The behavior isn't a security failure. It's a distribution failure. People reach for an AI tool when the alternative — finding the policy, the deadline, the leadership update, the new benefits doc — takes longer or never lands. Frame this as the unflattering version of an open-rate problem: the unread message that drove the AI search.

**H2 — Governance is what the category sells when it can't sell reach**

The pitch reads cleanly to IT. Auditing, blocking, restricting, approved-tools lists. It does not read to the IC director, the chief of staff, or the people leader who owns the actual employee experience of this. None of those titles have the budget, the mandate, or the appetite to police what employees type into a browser. They have a mandate to communicate.

(Optional sub-point: the 79% stat — quoted as something the category is moving on — but reframed as evidence that the channel isn't trusted, not that employees are misbehaving.)

**H2 — The reframe: make the sanctioned channel the first place people look**

This is the move that actually fixes the behavior. Not block-and-restrict. Build the channel employees go to first because it's faster, more relevant, and actually answers the question. Concrete: targeted by role, delivered where they already are (Outlook, Gmail, Teams, mobile), measurable so you know which messages are landing and which aren't.

The honest version: if your sanctioned channel is a 6,000-word all-hands newsletter that nobody opens, your employees will keep asking ChatGPT for the deadline. The fix is the channel, not the chatbot policy.

**H2 — What to do this quarter (without buying a control panel)**

Three concrete moves IC can make without an IT project:

1. Audit the questions employees keep asking AI tools. (Your help desk and your manager Slack channels know. Ask.) Those are the messages your channel isn't landing.
2. Pick the top three. Rewrite as targeted, segmented sends — to the people who need each one. Not org-wide. Targeted.
3. Measure what got read. The reason the AI tool wins is that it answers the question. The reason your message wins is that the right person reads it. You can't claim the second without a number.

**H2 — Where Foundations fits**

Soft landing. Foundations is free. You can stand up a sanctioned channel — targeted, branded, measurable — in an afternoon without IT. That's the proof that this isn't a $50K-and-two-quarters problem anymore. If the category wants to sell IT a control panel, fine. The IC team has a faster move.

Close on the forward takeaway: the vendors leading with shadow AI in ad creative are answering the wrong question. The right question is "why didn't the employee come to us first," and the answer is almost always the channel.
