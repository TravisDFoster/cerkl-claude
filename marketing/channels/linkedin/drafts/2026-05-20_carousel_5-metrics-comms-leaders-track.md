## Slide 1 — Cover
**5 metrics every internal communicator should track**
*The ones leadership cares about.*

## Slide 2 — Reach by segment
Open rate tells you who clicked. Reach by segment tells you who you missed.

If your warehouse team is at 18% and your HQ is at 72%, the average looks fine. The gap is the story.

**Track:** % of intended audience reached, broken out by segment.

## Slide 3 — Acknowledgment rate
For anything compliance-critical, "they got the email" isn't enough.

Acknowledgment data is the document that proves the organization met its obligation.

**Track:** % of required audience that acknowledged receipt, by send type.

## Slide 4 — HR ticket volume after critical sends
A spike in HR tickets after a benefits send means the message didn't land.

It's also the cost. Every repeat question is staff time the comms team could have prevented with a targeted re-send.

**Track:** HR support tickets per send, week-over-week.

## Slide 5 — Coverage on your top sends
Not every send matters equally. The ones leadership funds are compliance, safety, and benefits.

For your top 5 sends each quarter, measure who actually received them. That's where the case for investment lives.

**Track:** Coverage % by send for compliance, safety, and benefits messaging.

## Slide 6 — Engagement trend over time
A single open rate is a snapshot. A 12-month trend is a story.

Before/after comparisons turn data into a causal argument leadership can evaluate.

**Track:** Quarter-over-quarter engagement by segment, with annotations for major program changes.

## Slide 7 — Payoff
Stop reporting activity.
Start quantifying outcomes.

That's the shift that gets comms budget approved.
